Safety pact
United for safer rides

inDrive
Passengers
Drivers

We want all of us to be on the same page about safety

And so, we're calling this page our safety pact – a 3-sided alliance between passengers, drivers and inDrive, with our mutual responsibilities for every single ride

«Our customers’ safety is as important to inDrive as ensuring fair play in business and equal opportunity for all»

Passengers' side
Back seat responsibility goes far beyond the safety belt

Driver verification

Document and photo-verification is a must for all our drivers. If the driver or car doesn't match the profile in the app, the passenger should cancel the ride and contact the support team

Choose your driver or passenger

With inDrive, passengers always get to pick their drivers. They can check driver rating, car model, and ride history, all before accepting the offer. This goes both ways: drivers are free to choose passengers based on their profiles, routes and price offers as well

Safety button

The shield icon on the main screen is for quickly contacting support, calling the police or an ambulance

Support

Whether you're a driver or a passenger, to report a violation or dispute please get in touch with our support team. Contact support@indriver.com or via the in-app chat

Share your ride

Both passengers and drivers can share their ride details with friends or family. They'll see the route, info on driver or passenger and vehicle, as well as real-time trip status

Trusted contact

In the app, everyone can save the phone number of a trusted person for quick contact in an emergency. Trusted numbers can be added at any time, even during the ride

Rate your driver or passenger

Remember to rate your ride, whether you're a driver or a passenger. If you don't want to ride with that person again, give them one star and tap 'Don't Show My Requests'. They won't be able to see your ride requests/offers in the future

Passengers' side
Back seat responsibility goes far beyond the safety belt

Respect for all

Please respect your driver, their car, personal space, and privacy. Remember to greet your driver and thank them for the ride and any help with your luggage. Little things like this make your mutual experience much better

One Person – One Account

Please do not share your inDrive account with others. When using your account, you are responsible for your actions. It also helps our drivers recognize you more easily

Discrimination

We have a zero-tolerance policy towards discrimination of any kind. Please treat your driver with dignity and respect

Prohibited Substances

Please do not consume or transport alcohol (in open containers) or illegal drugs while using our service

Riding with Kids

Children and under-18s must always be accompanied by an adult when using inDrive. If you're traveling with a child, let us know in advance if you need a child seat

Riding with Pets

Let the driver know beforehand if you're bringing a pet. Please bring a carrier or a blanket to cover the car seat and reduce the risk of mess

Passengers' safety tips

Before the ride




1
Check the license plate, car model, and driver photo before starting a trip
2
Cancel the ride if the info doesn't match.
3
Let us know the entrance number or precise pick-up location, and double-check the destination address when you book. If you have more than 4 passengers, bulky luggage or a pet, please mention this in the comments section.
4
Changed your mind? Let the driver know and cancel your trip.

During the ride




1
Don't forget to buckle up.
2
Please inform your driver if you want to change your destination or add stops. Note that this is likely to change your fare
3
Treat your driver's vehicle with care: don't smoke, eat, or drink anything but water in the car. Feel free to ask the driver to change the music, adjust the temperature, or close the window. Just make sure your request works for both of you.
4
Tap the shield icon on the main screen to contact emergency services.
5
Use the same button to share your ride details with a friend or family member.

After the ride




1

Remember to rate your ride. Your feedback helps us understand your experience and improve the service. To help prevent any disagreements, try to avoid criticizing the driver during the ride. If you give your driver one star, they'll no longer see your ride requests in their feed.

2

Left an item in the car? Please get in touch with our support team via email at support@indrive.com or through the in-app chat.

Drivers' side
Your professionalism, well-being and even mood all matter

Fatigue is a red flag

Drivers are responsible for the safety of any ride they take. It's impossible to concentrate on the road without high-quality and timely rest – so please do not drive more than 10 hours in a row. Take breaks whenever you need them. inDrive offers the fairest conditions for drivers; there's no need to overdo it

One Person – One Account

Please, do not share your inDrive account with others. When using your account, you are responsible for your actions. It also helps our passengers recognize you more easily

No discrimination

We have a zero-tolerance policy against discrimination of any kind. Please, treat your passenger with dignity and respect

Prohibited Substances

Please do not consume or transport alcohol (in open containers) or illegal drugs while using our service

Riding with Kids

Children and under-18s must always be accompanied by an adult when using inDrive. If you accept a ride with a child, please prepare a child seat in advance

Riding with Pets

Let the passenger know beforehand if you're allergic or intolerant to pets. To avoid any possible inconvenience for both of you, do not accept orders with pets

Drivers' safety tips

Before the ride




1
Fatigue behind the wheel is more dangerous than you think – please do not take orders if you feel tired or unwell.
2
Please double-check the final destination or pick-up point before accepting the order. If you feel unsafe to go somewhere, it’s better to skip this offer.
3
Check the passenger’s photo before starting a trip. Cancel the ride if the info doesn't match.
4
Changed your mind? Let the passenger know and cancel your trip.

During the ride




1
Don't forget to buckle up.
2
Please inform your passenger if you need to refuel the car – it's better to double-check the oil in advance.
3
Treat your passenger with care: don't smoke, eat, or drink anything but water in the car.
4
Be ready to change the music, adjust the temperature, or close the window. Just make sure the request works for both of you. Tap the shield icon on the main screen to contact emergency services.
5
Use the same button to share your ride details with a friend or family member.

After the ride




1

Remember to rate your ride. Your feedback helps us understand your experience and improve the service.

2

To help prevent any disagreements, try to avoid criticizing the passenger during the ride. If you give your passenger one star, they'll no longer see your offer requests in their feed.

3

Found a forgotten item in the car? Please get in touch with our support team via email at support@indrive.com or through the in-app chat.

inDrive safety initiatives around the globe

Standing for safer streets in Egypt

InDrive illuminates the streets of Cairo and Alexandria with special billboards, to help people feel safe in their neighborhoods at night. Especially while waiting for their cars

Women safety in Mexico is a matter of training

3 out of 4 women have experienced some type of violence or harassment in public transport. inDrive came up with an educational program for drivers

Pitstop for drivers in Pakistan

inDrive drivers deserve safe, inviting spaces where they can take a break, relax, communicate with each other, join educational programs and ask for assistance or support
